Why Amsterdam?

Amsterdam, the city of canals

Amsterdam is known as the world's most water-rich city, with its canals and harbors covering nearly a quarter of its surface. These waterways have been the city's lifeblood, shaping its character and prosperity.

The 17th-century Canal Belt, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2011, offers a glimpse into Amsterdam's rich history, while the medieval city center is currently undergoing extensive renovations. Along every canal, you'll find historic landmarks, significant sites, and captivating views waiting to be discovered.
